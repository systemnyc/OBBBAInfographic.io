The Fiscal Equation: A Massive Rebalancing Act

The OBBBA is built on a foundation of massive tax cuts and new defense spending, paid for by deep cuts to social programs and a substantial increase in federal borrowing. This chart breaks down the key financial components over the next decade.

The Human Impact: Who Gains and Who Loses?

Non-partisan analysis reveals a starkly regressive impact. When tax changes are combined with benefit cuts, the legislation results in a net financial loss for the poorest households, while the wealthiest see significant gains.

The Healthcare Toll: A Shrinking Safety Net

The bill's largest spending cuts target Medicaid, leading to a projected loss of health insurance for millions. This not only affects individual well-being but also places financial strain on hospitals and the broader economy.

Impact on Wages & Salaries

The bill creates competing pressures on American paychecks. While investment incentives could boost wages in some sectors over the long term, the immediate impact on the broader labor market is less certain.

Potential for Wage Growth 📈

The bill's deep corporate tax cuts and 100% expensing provisions are designed to incentivize business investment. Economic theory suggests that over several years, this could lead to higher productivity and increased demand for skilled labor, potentially driving up wages in sectors like manufacturing, tech, and energy.

Downward Pressure on Paychecks 📉

For many workers, especially in lower-wage jobs, the loss of benefits like affordable healthcare could effectively act as a pay cut. Increased financial insecurity weakens workers' bargaining power, and businesses may face less pressure to increase wages to attract talent if the public safety net is smaller.
